The 8% Pricing Secret

In Supermarket Simulator, pricing isn't just about profit; it's about Store Points (SP). If you price too high, customers complain and you lose SP, slowing your progress.

The Pro Strategy: Set all items to exactly $0.10 to $0.20 below the nearest round dollar amount after adding an 8% markup. This keeps customers happy while maximizing your margins.
Item Type Recommended Markup Customer Reaction
Essentials (Milk, Bread) 5-7% Highly Satisfied
Luxury (Alcohol, Steaks) 10-12% Acceptable
Cleaning Supplies 8% Optimal

Optimal Store Layout

Maximize your "Customer Per Minute" (CPM) by reducing walking distance. Place your most expensive licenses (like the Fridge/Freezer sections) toward the back of the store to force customers to walk past "impulse buy" dry goods.

The Check-Out Buffer

Never place shelves directly against the checkout counters. Leave at least 3 grid squares of space to prevent AI pathing issues, which can lead to "ghost queues" where customers get stuck and leave without paying.

When to Hire?

Hiring too early is the #1 reason for bankruptcy in Supermarket Simulator. Follow this timeline:

  • Level 1-10: Solo play. Focus on building a $2,000 cash reserve.
  • Level 15+: Hire your first Cashier. This allows you to focus 100% on ordering and stocking.
  • Storage Expansion: Only hire a Stocker once you have at least 4 racks in your storage room. Without racks, stockers are useless.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I pay off loans faster?

Only take the $500 loan early on. The $5,000 loan should be reserved for purchasing the "Alcohol License," which provides the highest ROI in the game.

Why are my shelves empty even with a stocker?

Check your storage room. If a box is on the floor and not on a rack, the stocker may ignore it. Always keep your backroom organized!
